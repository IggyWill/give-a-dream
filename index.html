Amahle's Story

Amahle wakes up at 5am every day to begin her 6km walk to school. Her mother, Lindiwe, lost her job at a local farm 18 months ago and the family relies on a small vegetable garden and a neighbour's support to survive.

Despite arriving exhausted, Amahle's teacher describes her as "the most determined learner in class." She received top marks in her literacy test but has been wearing the same torn shoes for two years. Her backpack is a plastic shopping bag.

With your support, Amahle can arrive at school with the tools she needs to learn — and the dignity every child deserves.

Transparency

The Trust Stack

Seven layers of accountability that make Give A Dream the most trusted education platform in Africa.

1
School Verification (3-Tier)

Tier 3: Document check. Tier 2: Remote video audit. Tier 1: In-person field agent visit. All verified schools display their tier badge on every profile.

2
Spending Accountability

Schools must submit receipts for every rand spent within 30 days. AI cross-checks amounts against regional price databases. No receipt = no next payment.

3
Blockchain Anchoring

Every donation is recorded on the Polygon blockchain within 24 hours. The transaction hash is visible on your receipt — verifiable by anyone, forever.

4
AI Fraud Detection

Continuous ML scoring of every school, transaction, and user. Duplicate photo detection, price anomaly flagging, and velocity monitoring run 24/7.

5
POPIA-Compliant Child Privacy

No surnames, no location data beyond province. All student data managed under South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act.

🇿🇦

South African Legal Compliance

Built from the ground up for the South African legal and regulatory environment.

NPO Act 71 of 1997

Give A Dream Foundation NPC is registered under the Non-Profit Organisations Act 71 of 1997. We file annual financial statements with the Department of Social Development. NPO Registration No: 123456/21.

Section 18A — Income Tax Act 58 of 1962

Give A Dream Foundation holds PBO status approved by SARS under Section 18A. Donors receive an automatically generated Section 18A certificate for each donation, enabling deductions of up to 10% of taxable income.

POPIA — Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013

All personal data is processed in compliance with POPIA. Learner profiles display first names only. Written parental/guardian consent is obtained and stored for every learner profile.

B-BBEE Socio-Economic Development (SED)

Corporate donations through Give A Dream qualify as SED spend under the B-BBEE Codes of Good Practice (Element 8). We issue a B-BBEE SED Evidence Pack accepted by all major verification agencies.

Children's Act 38 of 2005

All learner profile creation follows the best interest of the child principle. Written consent from a parent or guardian is required before any profile goes live. All donor communications are mediated through the school and Give A Dream moderation team.
